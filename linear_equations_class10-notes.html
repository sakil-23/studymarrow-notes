1. What is a Linear Equation in Two Variables?

An equation involving two variables, each raised to the first power (degree 1), is called a linear equation in two variables. The general form is:

A single linear equation in two variables has infinitely many solutions, because for every value of x you choose, there is a corresponding value of y that satisfies the equation. When plotted on the coordinate plane, all these solutions together form a straight line.

4. Methods of Solving a Pair of Linear Equations

There are four main methods for finding the solution of a pair of linear equations algebraically and graphically:

Method 1: Graphical Method

Plot both equations as straight lines on the same coordinate plane. The coordinates of the point where they intersect represent the solution of the pair.

1
Rearrange each equation in the form y = mx + c to identify easy-to-plot points.
2
Choose at least two values of x for each equation and calculate the corresponding y values.
3
Plot both lines on the same graph and read off the coordinates of the intersection point.
тЪа This method can give approximate answers when the intersection point has non-integer coordinates. Algebraic methods are preferred for exact solutions.

Method 2: Substitution Method

Express one variable in terms of the other using one equation, then substitute that expression into the second equation to reduce it to a single-variable equation.

1
From either equation, isolate one variable (e.g., write x = ... or y = ...).
2
Substitute this expression into the other equation тАФ it now has only one variable.
3
Solve this single-variable equation to find its value.
4
Substitute the found value back into the expression from Step 1 to find the other variable.
Worked Example

Solve:   x + y = 5  тАж(i)   and   2x тИТ 3y = 5  тАж(ii)

From (i):   x = 5 тИТ y

Substituting in (ii):   2(5 тИТ y) тИТ 3y = 5  тЖТ  10 тИТ 5y = 5  тЖТ  y = 1

Then:   x = 5 тИТ 1 = 4

тИ┤ Solution: x = 4, y = 1

Method 3: Elimination Method

Make the coefficients of one variable equal in both equations by multiplying by suitable constants, then add or subtract the equations to eliminate that variable.

1
Multiply one or both equations by appropriate non-zero constants so that one variable has the same coefficient in both equations.
2
Add or subtract the two equations to cancel out that variable.
3
Solve the resulting equation in one variable.
4
Substitute the found value back into either original equation to find the other variable.
Worked Example

Solve:   3x + 4y = 10  тАж(i)   and   2x тИТ 4y = 0  тАж(ii)

6. Equations Reducible to a Pair of Linear Equations

Some equations that are not linear in appearance can be converted into a pair of linear equations using a suitable substitution. This is also called the method of reducing to linear form.

Common trick: If equations contain terms like 1/x and 1/y, substitute:
p = 1/x   and   q = 1/y
Rewrite both equations in terms of p and q тАФ they become linear. Solve for p and q, then find x = 1/p and y = 1/q.
Worked Example

Solve:   2/x + 3/y = 13   and   5/x тИТ 4/y = тИТ2

Let p = 1/x,   q = 1/y. The equations become:

2p + 3q = 13  тАж(i)   and   5p тИТ 4q = тИТ2  тАж(ii)

Solving this linear pair (by elimination):   p = 2,   q = 3

тИ┤   x = 1/p = 1/2   and   y = 1/q = 1/3

тИ┤ Solution: x = 1/2, y = 1/3

Similarly, if equations involve expressions like (ax + b) or (cx + d) in the denominators, substitute the entire expression as a new variable to reduce to linear form, solve, and then reverse the substitution.

7. Word Problems тАФ Strategy

Most real-life application problems in this chapter require you to form a pair of linear equations from a given situation and then solve it. The standard approach is:

1
Read carefully and identify the two unknown quantities. Assign them variables, typically x and y.
2
Formulate two equations from the two conditions given in the problem.
3
Choose a suitable solving method and find the values of x and y.
4
Verify your answer by substituting back into both original conditions from the problem.
Common types of word problems in this chapter:   Age problems ┬╖ Speed-distance-time ┬╖ Two-digit number problems ┬╖ Mixture and coin problems ┬╖ Geometry (perimeter, area) ┬╖ Boat and stream (upstream-downstream) problems.
