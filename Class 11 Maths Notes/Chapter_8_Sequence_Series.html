Chapter 8: Sequences & Series

The ultimate guide to recognizing patterns: Arithmetic, Geometric, Harmonic progressions, Special series sums, and competitive inequalities.

Part 1

Sequences vs. Series

A Sequence is an ordered list of numbers arranged according to a specific mathematical rule. When the elements of a sequence are connected by addition (+) signs, the resulting expression is called a Series.

1, 3, 5, 7

Sequence (Progression)

A list of terms denoted as a₁, a₂, a₃... aₙ. If the number of terms is limited, it's a finite sequence. If it goes on forever, it's an infinite sequence.

1 + 3 + 5 + 7

Series (Summation)

The sum of the terms of a sequence, denoted as Sₙ = a₁ + a₂ + ... + aₙ. We use the Greek letter Sigma (Σ) as a compact notation for summation.

Part 2

Arithmetic Progression (A.P.)

An A.P. is a sequence where the difference between any consecutive terms is a constant. This constant is called the Common Difference (d).
Format: a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d...

Key Formulas for A.P. :

• General (nth) Term: Tₙ = a + (n - 1)d
• Sum of n terms: Sₙ = (n/2) × [2a + (n - 1)d]
• Sum of n terms (if last term 'l' is known): Sₙ = (n/2) × (a + l)
• Finding nth term from Sum: Tₙ = Sₙ - Sₙ₋₁

📈 Arithmetic Mean (A.M.)

If three numbers a, A, b are in A.P., then A is the Arithmetic Mean of a and b.

A = (a + b) / 2

Note: You can insert 'n' Arithmetic Means between two numbers 'a' and 'b'. The common difference will be d = (b - a) / (n + 1).

Part 3

Geometric Progression (G.P.)

A G.P. is a sequence where the ratio of any term to its preceding term is a constant. This constant is called the Common Ratio (r).
Format: a, ar, ar², ar³...

Growth Comparison
A.P. grows linearly (+d), while G.P. grows exponentially (×r).
Key Formulas for G.P. :

• General (nth) Term: Tₙ = a · rⁿ⁻¹
• Sum of n terms (if r > 1): Sₙ = a(rⁿ - 1) / (r - 1)
• Sum of n terms (if r < 1): Sₙ = a(1 - rⁿ) / (1 - r)
• Sum of Infinite G.P. (only if |r| < 1): S∞ = a / (1 - r)

📊 Geometric Mean (G.M.)

If three numbers a, G, b are in G.P., then G is the Geometric Mean of a and b.

G = √(ab)

Note: The product of 'n' Geometric Means inserted between a and b is equal to the nth power of the single G.M. between them: (√ab)ⁿ.

Part 4 (Advanced)

Harmonic Progressions & Inequalities

Competitive exams (like JEE) heavily feature Harmonic Progressions (H.P.) and the relationships between the three standard means.

Harmonic Progression (H.P.)

A sequence is an H.P. if the reciprocals of its terms form an A.P.

If a₁, a₂, a₃ is an H.P., then 1/a₁, 1/a₂, 1/a₃ is an A.P.

Note: There is no general formula for the sum of an H.P.

Harmonic Mean (H.M.)

If a, H, b are in H.P., then H is the Harmonic Mean.

H = 2ab / (a + b)

The Master Inequality
Equality holds true only when a = b.
The Golden Relation between A.M., G.M., and H.M.:

For any two positive distinct real numbers 'a' and 'b':
1. A.M. > G.M. > H.M.
2. The G.M. is exactly the geometric mean of A.M. and H.M.: (G.M.)² = A.M. × H.M.
Part 5 (Advanced)

A.G.P. & Special Series

🔄 Arithmetico-Geometric Progression (A.G.P.)

A sequence formed by multiplying the corresponding terms of an A.P. and a G.P. together.

Format: a, (a+d)r, (a+2d)r², (a+3d)r³...

  • Sum to Infinity (|r| < 1):
    S∞ = a / (1 - r) + dr / (1 - r)²

Sum of Special Sequences

You must memorize these three standard summations to solve complex series problems using the Method of Differences.

1. Sum of first n natural numbers (Σn):
1 + 2 + 3 + ... + n = n(n + 1) / 2

2. Sum of squares of first n natural numbers (Σn²):
1² + 2² + 3² + ... + n² = n(n + 1)(2n + 1) / 6

3. Sum of cubes of first n natural numbers (Σn³):
1³ + 2³ + 3³ + ... + n³ = [ n(n + 1) / 2 ]² = (Σn)²
  • Method of Differences: If the sequence itself is not an AP or GP, but the differences between its consecutive terms form an AP or GP, you can find the general term Tₙ by writing out the sum Sₙ twice, shifting the second row by one position, and subtracting. Once Tₙ is found, use ΣTₙ to find the total sum.
