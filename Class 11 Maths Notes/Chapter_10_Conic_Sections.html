Chapter 10: Conic Sections

The ultimate guide mastering geometric loci: Circles, Parabolas, Ellipses, Hyperbolas, Parametric coordinates, and Tangency rules.

Part 1

The Foundation: Locus & Eccentricity

Geometrically, conic sections are curves obtained by intersecting a right circular cone with a plane. Analytically, a conic section is the locus of a point P which moves such that the ratio of its distance from a fixed point (Focus) to its perpendicular distance from a fixed straight line (Directrix) is a constant.

The Master Definition
The constant ratio 'e' is called the Eccentricity.
e = 0

Circle

Focus acts as the center, and the directrix is effectively at infinity.

e = 1

Parabola

Distance to focus is exactly equal to the distance to the directrix.

0 < e < 1

Ellipse

A closed curve where the sum of distances from two foci is constant.

e > 1

Hyperbola

An open curve with two branches extending to infinity.

🔥 General Equation of Second Degree (JEE Core)

Every conic section can be represented by the general equation:
ax² + 2hxy + by² + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0

To determine which conic it is, first calculate the discriminant Δ = abc + 2fgh - af² - bg² - ch². For a true conic curve, Δ ≠ 0.

  • If h² = ab ⇒ Parabola
  • If h² < ab ⇒ Ellipse (If a=b and h=0, it's a Circle)
  • If h² > ab ⇒ Hyperbola (If a+b=0, it's a Rectangular Hyperbola)
Part 2

Circles

Standard Form:
(x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²    (Center at (h, k), radius = r)

General Form:
x² + y² + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0
• Center = (-g, -f)
• Radius = √(g² + f² - c)

Diametric Form:
If ends of a diameter are (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂):
(x - x₁)(x - x₂) + (y - y₁)(y - y₂) = 0
Part 3

Parabolas

A parabola is a symmetrical open plane curve formed by the intersection of a cone with a plane parallel to its side. (Eccentricity e = 1).

Standard Parabola (y² = 4ax)
Opens rightwards. Focus lies on the positive X-axis.
Equation Focus Directrix Vertex Latus Rectum Length
y² = 4ax (a, 0) x = -a (0, 0) 4a
y² = -4ax (-a, 0) x = a (0, 0) 4a
x² = 4ay (0, a) y = -a (0, 0) 4a
x² = -4ay (0, -a) y = a (0, 0) 4a
Part 4

Ellipses

An ellipse is the locus of a point such that the sum of its distances from two fixed points (the foci) is constant. (0 < e < 1).

Standard Ellipse (x²/a² + y²/b² = 1)
Where a > b. Major axis lies along X-axis.
Key Formulas for Standard Ellipse (a > b):

• Eccentricity Relation: b² = a²(1 - e²)  ⇒  e = √(1 - b²/a²)
• Foci: (±ae, 0)
• Vertices: (±a, 0)
• Directrices: x = ±a/e
• Length of Major Axis: 2a  |  Length of Minor Axis: 2b
• Length of Latus Rectum: 2b² / a
Part 5

Hyperbolas

A hyperbola is the locus of a point such that the absolute difference of its distances from two fixed points (foci) is constant. (e > 1).

Standard Equation: x² / a² - y² / b² = 1

• Eccentricity Relation: b² = a²(e² - 1)  ⇒  e = √(1 + b²/a²)
• Transverse Axis: Length = 2a (analogous to major axis)
• Conjugate Axis: Length = 2b (analogous to minor axis)
• Foci: (±ae, 0)
• Vertices: (±a, 0)
• Directrices: x = ±a/e
• Length of Latus Rectum: 2b² / a

Conjugate Hyperbola

Formed by swapping the transverse and conjugate axes.

y² / b² - x² / a² = 1

Rectangular Hyperbola

A special hyperbola where the transverse and conjugate axes are equal in length (a = b). The eccentricity is always e = √2.

x² - y² = a²  or  xy = c²

Part 6 (Advanced)

Parametric Forms & Tangency (JEE Level)

Conic Section Parametric Coordinates (x, y) Parameter
Circle (x²+y²=r²) (r cos θ, r sin θ) θ (0 to 2π)
Parabola (y²=4ax) (at², 2at) t ∈ ℝ
Ellipse (x²/a² + y²/b² = 1) (a cos θ, b sin θ) θ (0 to 2π)
Hyperbola (x²/a² - y²/b² = 1) (a sec θ, b tan θ) θ (0 to 2π)
Rectangular (xy=c²) (ct, c/t) t ∈ ℝ - {0}

🎯 The Condition of Tangency Cheat Sheet

For the straight line y = mx + c to be a tangent to the conic curve, the specific condition for 'c' must be met:

  • Circle (x²+y²=a²):   c² = a²(1 + m²)
  • Parabola (y²=4ax):   c = a / m
  • Ellipse (x²/a² + y²/b² = 1):   c² = a²m² + b²
  • Hyperbola (x²/a² - y²/b² = 1):   c² = a²m² - b²
