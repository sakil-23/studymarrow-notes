Chapter 9: Straight Lines

The ultimate guide to mastering 2D coordinates, slope dynamics, various line equations, distance formulas, and competitive family of lines.

Part 1

Coordinate Geometry Basics (Recap)

Before diving into lines, we must solidify the foundational formulas of points in the Cartesian plane. Let P(x₁, y₁) and Q(x₂, y₂) be two points.

Distance Formula

The absolute distance d between points P and Q is given by:

d = √[ (x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)² ]

Section Formula

The coordinates of a point R dividing the line segment PQ internally in the ratio m:n are:

( (mx₂ + nx₁)/(m+n) , (my₂ + ny₁)/(m+n) )

*For external division, replace '+' with '-'.

Area of a Triangle

For a triangle with vertices (x₁, y₁), (x₂, y₂), and (x₃, y₃):

Area = ½ | x₁(y₂ - y₃) + x₂(y₃ - y₁) + x₃(y₁ - y₂) |

*If Area = 0, the three points are collinear.

📐 Triangle Centers (Competitive Core)

For a triangle with vertices A(x₁, y₁), B(x₂, y₂), C(x₃, y₃) and side lengths a, b, c:

  • Centroid (G): Intersecting point of medians.
    G = ( (x₁+x₂+x₃)/3 , (y₁+y₂+y₃)/3 )
  • Incenter (I): Intersecting point of internal angle bisectors.
    I = ( (ax₁+bx₂+cx₃)/(a+b+c) , (ay₁+by₂+cy₃)/(a+b+c) )
Part 2

Slope of a Line

The slope (or gradient) of a line determines its steepness and direction. It is denoted by m.

Angle of Inclination
θ is measured anti-clockwise from the positive X-axis.
  • From Angle: If a line makes an angle θ with the positive x-axis, its slope is m = tan θ (where θ ≠ 90°).
  • From Two Points: If a line passes through P(x₁, y₁) and Q(x₂, y₂), its slope is m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁).
  • Parallel Lines: Two non-vertical lines are parallel if and only if their slopes are equal (m₁ = m₂).
  • Perpendicular Lines: Two non-vertical lines are perpendicular if and only if the product of their slopes is -1 (m₁ · m₂ = -1).
Angle Between Two Lines:

If θ is the acute angle between two lines with slopes m₁ and m₂:

tan θ = | (m₂ - m₁) / (1 + m₁m₂) |

*If 1 + m₁m₂ = 0, the lines are perpendicular (θ = 90°).
Part 3

Forms of Equation of a Line

Depending on the given geometrical parameters, we use different standard forms to write the equation of a straight line.

1. Point-Slope Form

Line passing through point (x₁, y₁) with slope m:

y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)

2. Two-Point Form

Line passing through (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂):

y - y₁ = [ (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁) ] (x - x₁)

3. Slope-Intercept Form

Line with slope m and y-intercept c:

y = mx + c

4. Intercept Form
x/a + y/b = 1
Where 'a' is x-intercept, 'b' is y-intercept.
5. Normal Form
x cos(ω) + y sin(ω) = p
Where 'p' is length of normal from origin, and 'ω' is its angle.
General Equation of a Line:

Any line can be represented as: Ax + By + C = 0
• Slope (m) = -A / B
• X-intercept = -C / A
• Y-intercept = -C / B
Part 4

Distance Formulas

  • Distance of a Point from a Line: The perpendicular distance d of a point P(x₁, y₁) from the line Ax + By + C = 0 is:
    d = | Ax₁ + By₁ + C | / √(A² + B²)
  • Distance between Parallel Lines: The perpendicular distance d between two parallel lines Ax + By + C₁ = 0 and Ax + By + C₂ = 0 is:
    d = | C₁ - C₂ | / √(A² + B²)
Part 5 (Advanced)

Competitive Topics (JEE Level)

🎯 Translation of Axes (Shift of Origin)

If the origin is shifted to a new point (h, k) without rotating the axes, the relationship between the old coordinates (x, y) and new coordinates (X, Y) of any point is:

x = X + h
y = Y + k

📐 Family of Lines

The general equation of a family of lines passing through the point of intersection of two given lines L₁ = 0 and L₂ = 0 is given by:

L₁ + λL₂ = 0

(Where λ is an arbitrary real parameter determined by an additional given condition, like passing through a third point or having a specific slope).

Foot of Perpendicular & Image (Reflection):

If a perpendicular is dropped from point P(x₁, y₁) to the line Ax + By + C = 0, let the foot of the perpendicular be (x, y). The shortcut formula is:

(x - x₁) / A = (y - y₁) / B = - (Ax₁ + By₁ + C) / (A² + B²)

If (x, y) is the Image (Reflection) of P across the line, multiply the right side by 2:

(x - x₁) / A = (y - y₁) / B = -2(Ax₁ + By₁ + C) / (A² + B²)
